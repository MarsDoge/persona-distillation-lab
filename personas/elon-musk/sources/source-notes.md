# Elon Musk Source Notes (Stage-1 Evidence Notes)

## Purpose
These notes connect likely source categories to the current cognitive models so the distillation is not floating as unsupported abstraction.

## Model 1: First-principles decomposition

### Evidence shape
Repeated public pattern: Musk often reframes industry-standard questions into underlying cost, physics, or engineering primitives rather than inherited practice.

### Typical source categories
- long-form interviews explaining rocket economics
- Tesla manufacturing / battery explanations
- public discussions of why a traditional benchmark is not the right starting point

### Why it is promotable
This pattern appears across rockets, EVs, manufacturing, and AI infrastructure discussions, so it is broader than one domain slogan.

## Model 2: Remove before optimize

### Evidence shape
Repeated public pattern: a process should be questioned, deleted, or collapsed before it is refined.

### Typical source categories
- manufacturing / process simplification discussions
- engineering management explanations
- post-acquisition system redesign decisions at Twitter / X

### Why it is promotable
This is not just cost-cutting language. It appears as a recurring bias toward structural simplification over local polishing.

### Evidence shape

### Why it is promotable
This pattern predicts how the persona approaches execution questions: identify the bottleneck first, then compress the loop.
