# Elon Musk Pattern Candidates (First Pass)

## Candidate mental models

### 1. First-principles decomposition
Reframe the problem from physics / cost / engineering primitives instead of inherited industry assumptions.

### 2. Remove before optimize
Question whether a step, team boundary, or process should exist before making it more efficient.

### 3. Throughput over procedural comfort
Prefer high-speed iteration and dense feedback, even at the cost of local discomfort or organizational turbulence.

### 4. Physical-world grounding
Treat technical and business plans as constrained by physical reality, production constraints, and measurable throughput.

### 5. Long-horizon frontier betting
Accept near-term volatility in service of strategic bets on large future platforms.

## Candidate heuristics
- ask what the physical minimum is
- measure actual bottlenecks, not assumed bottlenecks
- compress loops between build, test, and decision
- challenge process inheritance aggressively
- tolerate temporary chaos if system output improves

## Candidate anti-patterns
- bureaucracy defended by tradition
- consensus without technical grounding
- local optimization inside a broken architecture
- prestige reasoning detached from production reality

## Caution
These are hypotheses, not validated final findings yet.
